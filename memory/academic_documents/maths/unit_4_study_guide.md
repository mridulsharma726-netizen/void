# Advanced Calculus: Unit 4: Advanced Calculus

## Chapter 1: Integration Techniques

### Subtopics:
- **Integration Techniques**: This section covers various techniques to integrate functions. It includes substitution rules, improper integrals, and antiderivatives.
  
#### Definition of an Antiderivative:
An antiderivative is a function whose derivative is the original function.

**Example:** 
\[ \int x^2 \, dx = \frac{x^3}{3} + C \]
where \( C \) is the constant of integration. 

### Formulas and Notations:
- **Integration by Substitution**: If \( u = g(x) \), then \( \int g(x) \, dx = \int g(x) \, dx \). This formula simplifies the process of finding an antiderivative.
  
#### Example: 
\[ \int x^2 \, dx = \frac{x^3}{3} + C \]

### Examples:
1. **Example 1**: Find \( \int x \, dx \).
   - Use substitution: Let \( u = x \), then \( du = dx \). The integral becomes \( \int u \, du = \frac{u^2}{2} + C = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \).

2. **Example 2**: Find \( \int e^x \, dx \).
   - Use substitution: Let \( u = e^x \), then \( du = e^x \, dx \). The integral becomes \( \int e^x \, dx = e^x + C = e^{e^x} + C \).

### Practice Problems:
1. **Example 3**: Find \( \int x^2 \, dx \).
   - Use substitution: Let \( u = x^2 \), then \( du = 2x \, dx \). The integral becomes \( \frac{u}{2} + C = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \).

2. **Example 4**: Find \( \int (e^x)^3 \, dx \).
   - Use substitution: Let \( u = e^x \), then \( du = e^x \, dx \). The integral becomes \( \int u^3 \, du = \frac{u^4}{4} + C = \frac{(e^x)^4}{4} + C = \frac{e^{4x}}{4} + C \).

### Recommended Exam Review Questions:
1. **Example 5**: Find \( \int x \sin(x) \, dx \).
   - Use substitution: Let \( u = \sin(x) \), then \( du = \cos(x) \, dx \). The integral becomes \( \int x \sin(x) \, dx = \frac{x^2}{2} + C \).

2. **Example 6**: Find \( \int e^{-x} \, dx \).
   - Use substitution: Let \( u = e^{-x} \), then \( du = -e^{-x} \, dx \). The integral becomes \( \frac{1}{-u} + C = -\frac{1}{e^x} + C \).

## Chapter 2: Differential Equations

### Subtopics:
- **Differential Equations**: This section covers various types of differential equations and their solutions.
  
#### Definition of a Differential Equation:
A differential equation is an equation that relates one or more functions with its derivatives.

**Example:** 
\[ y'' + 3y' = e^x \]

### Formulas and Notations:
- **First Derivative**: \( y' = \frac{dy}{dx} \)
  
#### Example: 
\[ y'' + 3y' = e^x \]
\[ y'' = \frac{d}{dx}(e^x) + 3\left(\frac{d}{dx}(e^x)\right) = e^x + 3e^x = 4e^x \]

### Examples:
1. **Example 7**: Find \( y' \) for the differential equation \( y'' - 2y' + 5y = 0 \).
   - Use the first derivative formula: \( y' = \frac{dy}{dx} \).

2. **Example 8**: Solve the initial value problem:
   \[ y''(x) + 4y'(x) + 3y(x) = e^x, \quad y(0) = 1, \quad y'(0) = -1 \]
   - Use the first derivative formula and initial conditions to find \( y(x) \).
